Frequently asked Questions

We know that there tends to be questions that are asked about the business or how certain things are done or how we can help you make good decisions on buying a vehicle or repairing a vehicle from us, so here are a few basic questions that are asked on a regualar basis.


Q:What is the best way to negotiate the price of a car?

A:The best way to negotiate the price of a car is to do your research, know the market value of the car, and be prepared to walk away if the deal is not good enough.
Q:Can I trade in my old car at a dealership?
A:Yes, most dealerships will accept trade-ins. The value of your trade-in will be applied to the purchase price of the new car.
Q:Can I return a car to a dealership if I change my mind?
A:Most dealerships have a return policy, but it may vary. Some dealerships may offer a full refund, while others may charge a restocking fee.
Q:Can I negotiate the price of a used car?
A:Yes, you can negotiate the price of a used car. Research the market value of the car and be prepared to make a case for why you think the price should be lower.
Q: What kind of documentation do I need to purchase a car from a dealership?
A: You will typically need to provide proof of insurance, a valid driver's license, and financing information. The dealership may also require additional documentation, such as proof of income or residency.
